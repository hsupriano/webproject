My Journey

Why did I decide to learn to code after years of ignoring my brothers advice? Well there were a couple different reasons. One is that my family has been living off of one income for years, and with 4 children in our house that keeps finances very tight. The other was me coming across a webinar on Facebook, it was introducing a beginners course for Python. I watched the webinar and got really excited. What the guy was talking about seemed so interested, so I ended up signing up for the course. I flew through the course in about half the average time and was very excited. However it was only a piece of the puzzle and I got frustrated because it didn't really show what to do next. From there I spent a lot of time watching videos trying out free coding sites, reading articles, talking to people, and just really trying to figure out what it was I should do next. I soon learned that I really first needed to decide what it is I wanted to focus on. Did I want to learn front-end web development, back-end web development, software development, or one of the other numerous types of development. In the end I decided to go with what made the most sense to start adding another income for my family the quickest, front-end web development. I have had what I like to call a bit of "Dory syndrome" trying to decide on what to learn and how to learn it. For every new language or learning method I saw my brain says "Oooh that seems interesting" and down a rabbit hole I go. Below are just a few of the rabbit holes I have wandered down.
